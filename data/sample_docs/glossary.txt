Bull Market: A market condition where stock prices are rising or expected to rise, characterized by investor optimism and confidence.

Bear Market: A market condition where stock prices are falling or expected to fall, often accompanied by investor pessimism.

P/E Ratio: The price-to-earnings ratio is a valuation metric calculated by dividing a company's current share price by its earnings per share.

Dividend: A portion of a company's earnings distributed to shareholders, usually as cash payments.

Diversification: An investment strategy of holding a variety of assets to reduce risk. Strong performance of some investments can offset poor performance of others.
